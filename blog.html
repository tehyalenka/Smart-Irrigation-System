Scope:

Adoption in agriculture: Smart irrigation systems can alter irrigation schedules and make sure that crops get the proper quantity of water at the right time by keeping an eye on soil moisture levels, weather patterns, and other environmental conditions.

Integration with IoT devices: To give more precise and current data, smart irrigation systems may be coupled with IoT devices like weather stations, soil moisture sensors, and flow metres. By making smarter judgements about when and how much water to apply to their crops or lawns, farmers and landscapers can conserve water.

Urban agriculture has gained popularity as cities continue to expand and the need for fresh vegetables rises. Even in small places, urban farmers may increase the efficiency of their crop growth and water use with the use of smart irrigation systems.

Home landscaping: To automate watering schedules, find leaks, and offer information on water consumption, smart irrigation systems may also be utilised for home landscaping.
